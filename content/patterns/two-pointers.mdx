## Core Intuition

Imagine a row of houses, each with a different number of people. A rescue boat drives past and can pick up a **fixed number of houses** at a time. Instead of recounting everyone from scratch each time the boat moves one house over, you **subtract the house you left** and **add the new house you entered**.

Two pointers work the same way on arrays: instead of nested loops checking every pair, you maintain two indices and move them based on a simple rule — cutting complexity from **O(n²) to O(n)**.

## Signal Words

Watch for these phrases in problem statements — they often hint at two pointers:

- "sorted array"
- "pair whose sum equals..."
- "remove duplicates in-place"
- "palindrome check"
- "container with most water"
- "minimum boats / pairs"

## Complexity

| Variant | Time | Space |
|---------|------|-------|
| Inward (sorted) | O(n) | O(1) |
| Inward (unsorted + sort) | O(n log n) | O(1) |
| Unidirectional | O(n) | O(1) |
